Why governance gets undervalued

Governance work often looks indirect. It does not always produce an immediately visible campaign lift or a dramatic chart.

Because the effect is indirect, teams underinvest until a high-stakes decision exposes the cost of inconsistency.

What good governance actually does

Good governance creates comparability. It makes a lead-source field mean the same thing across properties and keeps event names stable enough to reuse.

That means less time reconciling and more time answering better questions.

How governance becomes a growth function

Governance becomes a growth function when it changes the speed and quality of decisions. Better attribution, faster diagnosis, and cleaner budget moves all depend on the same discipline.

If the metric layer becomes more believable, the growth team becomes more decisive.
